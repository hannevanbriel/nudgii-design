For You: Card System

The discovery and engagement surface at the bottom of the Today tab. Cards are contextual, data-driven, and actionable. This is what makes nudgii feel alive rather than a static checklist — and it's the main reason to keep opening the app on a quiet day.

Display + rotation rules
How many cards show at once
Max 4 cards in the row at any time. Min 2 cards always present (savings summary + 1 other).

If the pool drops below 2 eligible cards, a generic "Did you know" fills the gap. The row never shows fewer than 2 cards.
Rotation + refresh
Weekly rotation for seasonal and editorial content, even if not dismissed.

On app open after 7+ days: rotate in fresh cards regardless of dismissal state.

Dismissed cards return after their timeout (7 or 30 days depending on type).
Dismissal behaviour
Tapping X dismisses a card immediately. It slides out of the row and the next eligible card slides in.

Dismissed duration: affiliate = 7 days, AI rec = 7 days, all others = 30 days.

Savings summary: no X button. Cannot be dismissed, ever.
Too empty — all sections empty
If TODAY + THIS WEEK + COMING UP are all empty: inject a persistent "Your schedule is quiet" card into FOR YOU. No X button. Body: "Add more items and nudgii will figure out the rest." CTA: + Add items.

This card disappears as soon as any task section has content.
Free vs Pro card quality
Free users see all 6 base card types. The type is the same, the intelligence is not.

Free: "Your boiler might need a service" (generic category tip).
Pro: "Your Vaillant ecoTEC Plus hasn't been serviced in 14 months — book before the warranty check window closes" (item-specific, Claude API).
Savings card — always first
Savings summary is always the first card in the row. It sets the emotional tone before the user sees suggestions.

On a quiet day: seeing "€340 saved" first makes the empty schedule feel like a reward, not a failure. It answers "why am I even using this?" without the user having to ask.
