Design direction · starting point

What made the celebration screen work.

Almost nothing is on screen. What IS there is precise: the ii mark, apricot on dark, DM Serif italic, empty space. That's the app's design voice. The question is how to carry it into every screen — not by copying the plum, but by keeping the same restraint.

The celebration screen works because one color dominates (plum), one accent glows (apricot), and the rest is empty. The ii mark is the whole design. Nothing competes with it. The daily app should feel the same — cream dominates, purple acts once, everything else is warm neutrals and good type.
01
Type is the design
Task names, dates, and category labels do all the visual work. No colored fills needed when the hierarchy is right.
02
Color as punctuation
Category color = a 6px dot. That's all. One dot, one hue per task. The cream card surface is the neutral it rests on.
03
Purple acts once
CTA, FAB, active state. Nowhere else in light mode. Because it's rare, it always reads as action. It never decorates.
04
Space is generous
More padding than feels comfortable. More gap between cards than expected. Apps that breathe feel considered, not rushed.
05
Apricot = warmth
In dark mode "Martijn." glows apricot like a candle. In the ii mark, it's the dot of life. Nowhere functional — only warm.
