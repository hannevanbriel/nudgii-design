Why it matters
A gas leak in a closed camper is life-threatening. Belgian law requires a valid gas certificate for campsite stays. Most insurers require annual inspection. This is the one task you never skip.
How to do it
1
Find a certified camper gas inspector (DEFA or equivalent)
2
Book 3-4 weeks ahead, spring is busy season
3
They check all connections, hoses, regulator, and appliances
4
Keep the certificate in the van, you'll need it at campsites

Edge cases tested
Cross-category hybrid
Camper van = vehicle + home. AI assigned "Vehicle" category (dominant function) but generated tasks from both domains: MOT + tyres (vehicle) AND gas check + water system + winterize (home/living). S-17 uses Vehicle variant with mileage tracker + countdown cards.
Task prioritization (12 > 5)
AI generated 12 possible tasks but the 5-task limit forced prioritization. Kept: gas safety (life-critical), MOT (legal), winterize (seasonal, prevents damage), water flush (health), tyre pressure (safety). Deprioritized: oil change, battery, fridge, roof seals, insurance, de-winterize, awning. User can add any of the 7 deprioritized tasks via "+ Add."
Safety guardrail
Gas system check is flagged "Certified technician" (apricot icon, pro badge). AI never suggests DIY gas work. The "Why it matters" explicitly mentions life-threatening risk and legal requirements. This is the content review queue in action.
Mixed cadences
Five different cadence types in one item: yearly (gas, MOT), seasonal (winterize), trip-based (water flush), monthly (tyres), and mileage-based (oil change in deprioritized list). The task system handles all of them.
Deprioritized tasks UX
New pattern: "7 more tasks available" collapsible section below the main 5. Each shows task name + "+ Add" button. User picks what matters to them. AI doesn't hide options, it ranks them. This solves the "but what about my oil change?" objection without overwhelming the review screen.
