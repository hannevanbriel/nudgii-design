Digest nudge strategy, conversion data
1. Digest nudge cards (Day 3-7)
★
Day 1: no nudge. Let the user enjoy the product. First impressions matter more than conversion speed. Bombarding new users on day 1 increases churn by 20% (Leanplum retention study).
N
Day 3: milestone framing. "Your first week is almost done." Curiosity-driven, not demanding. The user has had time to complete at least one task. Digest nudge appears as a card in the task list, not a modal or banner.
N
Day 5: achievement-based. "1 done. Want to see your progress?" Tied to actual completions. Users who complete at least one task in week 1 have 3x better D30 retention (industry benchmark for task/habit apps).
N
Day 7: earned humor. "Your dishwasher is taking notes." The nudgii voice at its best: warm, witty, never naggy. After day 7, no more digest nudges. nudgii doesn't nag. Three chances, then respect the answer.
↓
Two paths on tap: If signed in (happy path, 70-80% of users), tapping enables the digest instantly. Success toast: "Done. First overview lands Monday." If not signed in, tapping opens Apple/Google SSO bottom sheet. They sign in, digest enables automatically. One flow, no email form ever.
↑
Greeting reflects auth state. Signed in: "Good morning, Martijn." (name from Apple/Google profile). Skipped SSO: "Good morning, there." Name personalization becomes a subtle reward for signing in.
