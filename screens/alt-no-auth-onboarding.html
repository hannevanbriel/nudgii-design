Alternative flow: no-auth onboarding
What if users never see a signup wall? Instead of requiring auth during onboarding, users go straight from AHA to dashboard. nudgii then uses its own reminder system to nudge users to create an account. The signup nudge looks exactly like a maintenance reminder, because that is what nudgii does: it reminds you about things that matter.

Strategy notes, design decisions, conversion hypothesis
1. Value before commitment
★
The user never sees a signup wall. They experience the full product value first. Every nudge is a demonstration of what nudgii does: remind you about things that matter.
N
The signup nudge IS the product. It looks like a maintenance reminder, behaves like a maintenance reminder, and lives in the task feed alongside real reminders.
↓
Push permission moves earlier (critical). Without push, nudgii cannot deliver value OR signup nudges. This is the only gate in the flow.
2. Account nudge card design
N
Same visual as maintenance nudge cards. ii mark replaces category chip in a 22x22 circle with rgba(155,127,212,0.08) background. No left-border accent (per design rules).
↑
Border: rgba(155,127,212,0.18) instead of standard rgba(26,22,18,0.08). Subtle CTA-tinted border distinguishes it from task cards without breaking the card pattern.
↓
Card is tappable. Opens auth flow inline (bottom sheet or full screen). Same Apple/Google/email flow as current S-07.
3. Nudge timing
★
Day 1: No nudge. Let the user enjoy the product. First impressions matter more than conversion speed.
N
Day 3: Data protection framing. "Your schedule lives on this phone only." Practical, not pushy.
N
Day 5: Post-completion framing. "1 done. Want to keep the streak?" Achievement-based, tied to action.
N
Day 7: Personality framing. "Your dishwasher is taking notes." Witty nudgii voice. The earned joke.
↓
After day 7: No more nudges. If they haven't signed up, respect that. nudgii doesn't nag. One nudge, clearly stated. That rule applies to signup too.
4. What changes vs current flow
↑
S-07 Auth removed from onboarding entirely. No signup wall between AHA and dashboard.
↑
S-06 AHA CTA: "Open my schedule" replaces "Save my schedule". No "Free, no credit card, 30 seconds" sub-note needed.
N
Push permission screen added between AHA and dashboard. Critical for delivery of both task reminders and signup nudges.
N
New card type: account nudge. ii mark icon chip, same card style, CTA-tinted border. Auth flow triggered by tap.
↓
Greeting: "Good morning, there." instead of personalised name (no account = no name). Name personalisation becomes a post-signup reward.
5. The irony
★
nudgii reminds you about your boiler, your dishwasher, your tyres... and itself. The signup reminder IS a product demo. It is the most on-brand conversion tactic possible.
★
The card format proves nudgii works before the user commits. They see "Your schedule lives on this phone only" sitting right next to "Annual boiler service, Oct 28". Same format, same care, same system.
↓
If a user dismisses all 3 nudges and never signs up, they still got value. nudgii still reminded them about their boiler. That is the product philosophy: helpful first, commercial second.
6. Conversion hypothesis
★
Hypothesis: removing the signup wall increases activation (items added, tasks completed) at the cost of delayed account creation. Net effect: higher LTV because users who create accounts later have already proven product-market fit for themselves.
