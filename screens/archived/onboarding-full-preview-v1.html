Design decisions: why this flow order
★
Two-state screen (AHA + celebration)
Reveal and celebration are one screen with a spring transition. "That's it. I'm done." triggers the plum takeover. No misleading CTAs, no extra navigation step. The emotional peak and the action happen together.
🔒
Sign-in slides up over celebration
Bottom sheet on plum background. The celebration is still visible behind. "Save your schedule" frames sign-in as protecting something you just built, not passing through a gate.
🔔
Push shows actual first date
"We'll remind you on Oct 28" makes the permission ask concrete. Abstract asks ("stay on top of things") convert at 20-25%. Concrete asks convert at 60-70%.
✓
"Maybe later" never blocks
Both push choices lead to the dashboard. Denying push permission never blocks progression. Re-trigger fires contextually when first reminder is within 3 days.
▢
One fewer screen in the flow
v1 had 3 screens between schedule and dashboard (celebration, auth, push). v2 combines reveal + celebration + auth into one two-state screen. User reaches dashboard faster with no bait-and-switch.
💰
No limits during onboarding
Users can add unlimited items during onboarding. The 10-item free tier limit is enforced on the dashboard only. Items 11+ are visible but paused (no reminders). Users choose which 10 stay active, or upgrade. Conversion trigger: when a paused item's task comes due.
