Collection shelf
Apple Music library, Calm content cards
Core idea editorial
Items displayed as tall cards with a generous category-wash hero zone. Feels like browsing a curated collection, not checking a to-do list. The 2px accent line at the bottom signals status without visual clutter.
Best for Lena Sara
Users who enjoy the app as a calm, aesthetic experience. Each item feels like it matters. Discovery-oriented users (Lena) feel their home is a "collection" to care for, not a task list.
Trade-off
Spacious layout means fewer items visible without scrolling. Power users (Arne) with 15+ items may find it slow to scan. Consider: compress cards after 8 items?
Status board
Tiimo, Things 3, Linear
Core idea dashboard
Items grouped by status, not category. "Needs attention" floats to top with personality text. On-track items stay compact. Answers the daily question: "What needs me?" in one glance.
Best for Arne Martijn
Power users and busy homeowners who want signal, not beauty. Arne scans the attention section and is done in 3 seconds. Martijn gets the "all clear" feeling when attention section is empty.
Trade-off
Category becomes secondary. Users who organize mentally by "home stuff vs car stuff" may find it disorienting. Consider: allow toggle between status and category grouping?
Living grid
iOS Home app, Notion gallery, Arc
Core idea dynamic
The most urgent item gets a large featured card. Others fill tiles and rows. The layout changes based on what actually needs attention, making the screen feel alive across visits.
Best for Martijn Lena
Daily openers who want the app to feel different each time. The featured slot creates a "story of the day" for their home. Lena sees the item that most needs her learning attention.
Trade-off
Asymmetric layout is harder to build in Flutter (custom layout delegate). Items shift positions across visits, which may frustrate muscle-memory users. Consider: animate featured transitions?
